Paper Summary: DialogBench: Evaluating LLMs as Human-like Dialogue Systems by Ou et al.
---

Researchers in this study developed DialogBench, a novel tool designed to evaluate the ability of large language models (LLMs) to interact in human-like dialogues. In order to measure the capabilities of LLMs in emulating human dialogue, the researchers developed 12 dialogue tasks. They used the fourth iteration of GPT (GPT-4) to generate evaluation instances for each task.

The tasks evaluated various components of dialogue systems, such as their ability to understand dialogue context, use relevant knowledge, detect user emotions and personalities, and generate friendly and reasonable responses that are coherent with the dialogue context. The tasks were also designed around fine-grained abilities, leading to a more detailed and comprehensive evaluation of LLMS’s ability to mimic human behaviour.

The findings revealed an urgent need for improvement in the "human likeness" of most LLMs. It was shown that while instruction tuning does improve the likeness of LLMs to some extent, there is still significant room for improvement. For instance, while supervised instruction-tuning models generally outperformed their pre-tuned counterparts, a clear performance gap was observed between top-performing models like GPT-4 and the rest. Additionally, it was found that most LLMs performed better at understanding the context of a conversation, but struggled with perceiving emotions and personality traits.
 
To minimize biases, the researchers mitigated domain and dialogue style biases while designing the tasks. They also introduced a filter mechanism to select high-quality evaluation data. As a result, DialogBench was demonstrated as a useful tool for routinely evaluating and accelerating improvements in conversational AI.
